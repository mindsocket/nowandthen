{% trans "Welcome to Now and Then" %}

Now and Then is an application for combining historical images overlaid with modern equivalents. Each combination of an old and new image is called a "fusion". You can see existing fusions in the fusion gallery.

The site was created as an entry for the 2011 Libraryhack event.

{% trans "How do I contribute?" %}

Creating a new fusion is easy...
  • Browse the archive of historical images to find a suitable image. The collection currently covers pre-1855 images of Sydney, Australia.
  • Go to the location (with a reference copy of the historical image), and take a photo of the modern equivalent. Try to get as close to the original position and composition as possible.
  • Upload your image to Flickr and add it to the Now and Then group and/or tag it with nowandthen. Make sure that a permissive license (such as Creative Commons is used.
  • Return to the historical "then" image and create a new fusion, choosing your matching "now" image from Flickr.
  • Optionally, indicate the common points between the images so that the overlaid images are well aligned.
  • Voila! Your aligned fusion is ready for viewing.

What images can I contribute

Currently, while Now and Then is in a Beta release period, the image collection includes only "Photographs of Sydney before 1885" from the State Library of NSW. Other collections will hopefully be added at a later date.
