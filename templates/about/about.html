What's the Idea?

Now and Then is an application for viewing and contribute historical images overlaid with modern equivalents. Each combination of an old and new image is called a "fusion".

The application was created as an entry for the 2011 Libraryhack event.

How do I contribute?

Contributing a new fusion is easy... 1) Browse the archive of historical images to find a suitable image. The collection currently covers pre-1855 images of Sydney, Australia. 2) Go to the location (with a reference copy of the historical image), and take a photo of the modern equivalent. Try to get as close to the original position and composition as possible. 3) Upload your image to Flickr and add it to the Now and Then group and/or tag it with nowandthen. Make sure that a permissive license (such as Creative Commons is used. 4) Return to the historical "then" image and create a new fusion, choosing your matching "now" image from Flickr 5) Optionally, indicate the common points between the images so that the overlaid images are well aligned. 6) Voila! Your aligned fusion is ready for viewing.

What images can I contribute

Currently, while Now and Then is in a Beta release period, the image collection includes only "Photographs of Sydney before 1885" from the State Library of NSW. Other collections will hopefully be added at a later date.
